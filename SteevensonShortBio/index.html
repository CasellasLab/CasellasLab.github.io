Dr. Steevenson Nelson - Short Bio


Dr. Nelson received his undergraduate degree from the University of Massachusetts at Boston in 2001. Subsequently, he earned a doctorate in biochemistry from North Carolina State University in 2005 under the tutelage of Dr. Dennis Brown. His thesiswork focused on the production of truncated viral proteins for structural analysis and subunit vaccine production. He joined the laboratory of Dr. Ted Pierson at the NIH as a postdoctoral fellow in late 2005 where he investigated the impact of viral and cellular factors on antibody-mediated neutralization of Flavivirus infection. Currently, Dr. Nelson has a staff position in the laboratory of Dr. Rafael Casellas and is investigating factors that impact B-cell lymphoma development.
